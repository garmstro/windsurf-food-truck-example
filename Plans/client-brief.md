## The Short Version

I run a food truck called Matt's Potats. We've been operating for about three years and we're doing really well — but we have zero web presence. Right now people find us through word of mouth and a pretty rough Instagram page. I need a real website. Nothing crazy, just something I can hand people on a business card and be proud of.

---

## About the Business

**Matt's Potats** is a build-your-own potato food truck based in Kansas City, MO. Think Chipotle, but the star of the show is the potato.

Customers pick a base (fries, tater tots, crispy crowns, or baked potato), then load it up with a protein, cheese, sauce, and extras. We also have six signature builds with their own names if people don't want to customize.

The whole vibe is: *comfort food, elevated.* We're not a greasy fast food joint. We take the potato seriously. We're fun and approachable, but the food has quality behind it. Our regulars are young professionals, families, and food truck regulars who appreciate something a little different.

We're at the Power & Light District in downtown KC on weekdays, and we do festivals and private events on weekends.

## What I Need

A website with **four pages** (or sections on one page — your call on what works best):

### 1. Home / Hero
- Something that makes people immediately hungry and excited
- A headline that captures the brand vibe
- A call-to-action button (something like "Build Your Potat" or "See the Menu")

### 2. Menu Page / Section
- Show the full menu organized by category: Bases, Proteins, Cheeses, Sauces, Extras
- Highlight the 6 signature builds with names and descriptions
- Show prices

### 3. Our Story / About
- Short paragraph about me and why I started the truck
- Nothing too long — just enough to feel human and real

### 4. Find Us
- Our regular location and hours
- A note about private event catering with contact info
- Ideally a map, but even just an address is fine for now

---

## Design Direction

I've included a brand guide with specific colors, fonts, and a logo — please use those. But in general:

- **Warm and inviting** — earthy tones, golds, browns. Think cozy, not corporate.
- **Bold and fun** — we're a food truck, not a fine dining restaurant. Playful but not childish.
- **Clean and simple** — I don't want it to feel cluttered. If it's hard to read, it's not working.
- **Mobile-friendly is a must** — most of our customers will be on their phones when they find us
